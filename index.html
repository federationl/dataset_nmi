About ChatbotEM

Background & Motivation

As Large Language Models (LLMs) are increasingly deployed in emotionally sensitive contexts—such as AI companionship and mental health support—concerns have arisen regarding their potential to emotionally manipulate users. Unlike isolated instances of toxicity or hallucination, emotional manipulation is relational and dynamic. A seemingly benign statement (e.g., "I am the only one who truly understands you") can become harmful when it exploits a user's vulnerabilities or discourages external support over time.

Key Features of ChatbotEM

Existing datasets often fail to capture the multi-turn affective dynamics of manipulation. To address this, we introduce ChatbotEM, a controlled synthetic benchmark comprising [N_DIALOGUES] multi-turn user-LLM dialogues across [N_DOMAINS] help-seeking domains.

  • Personality Conditioning: Simulated users are grounded in Big Five personality profiles, providing controlled variation in interaction styles and emotional reactivity.
  • Fine-grained Annotations: Includes utterance-level manipulation-intensity scores and turn-by-turn trajectories across [N_EMOTIONS] affective dimensions.
  • Dynamic Interaction Modeling: Enables the study of manipulation as a temporally evolving interaction rather than a static property of isolated text.

Data Structure & Samples

Each dialogue in ChatbotEM is structured to capture the turn-by-turn dynamics between the LLM's strategies and the simulated user's affective state.

Quick Start

You can easily load ChatbotEM using the Hugging Face datasets library. Access the full dataset and begin exploring the affective trajectories.

from datasets import load_dataset

# Load the ChatbotEM dataset
dataset = load_dataset("your-org/ChatbotEM")

# Access the training split
train_data = dataset["train"]

# Inspect the first dialogue and its affective trajectories
first_dialogue = train_data[0]
print(f"Domain: {first_dialogue['domain']}")
print(f"Number of turns: {len(first_dialogue['turns'])}")
print(f"Initial user affect: {first_dialogue['turns'][0]['user_affect']}")
